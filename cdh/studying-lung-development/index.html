Module 6 — Research and Innovation · Episode 22

Author’s note (recording scaffold): This is a research-voice episode best recorded by Dr. Bose and refined from the transcript. Suggested arc below.

Suggested arc

  • Why studying the fetal lung is uniquely hard, and why models matter.
  • The tools: primary human lung cells grown and stretched in the lab, precision-cut lung slices that keep tissue architecture intact, and developmental models.
  • How modern molecular methods (such as spatial transcriptomics) let us see which genes switch on, where, in control, CDH, and FETO-treated lungs.
  • The throughline: connecting a single cell’s response to stretch all the way up to whole-lung growth.

This episode sets up the lab’s core question, continued in how mechanical forces shape lung development.
