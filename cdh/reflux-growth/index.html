Reflux and growth

Educational, not medical advice. If you have an urgent concern, call your physician or 911.

Module 5 — Long-Term Outcomes · Episode 19

Reflux is common

Gastroesophageal reflux is common after CDH, related to how the anatomy and pressures change after repair. It can affect comfort, feeding, and sometimes the lungs.

How it is managed

Management ranges from positioning and feeding adjustments to medicines, and, in some children, a surgical procedure to reduce reflux. The approach is tailored to severity.

Growth

Because feeding and reflux can limit intake, growth is watched closely, often with the help of a dietitian. Catch-up growth is common once feeding is established. Steady tracking, rather than any single weight, is what the team follows.
