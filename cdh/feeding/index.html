Feeding challenges after CDH

Educational, not medical advice. Feeding plans are individualized. If you have an urgent concern, call your physician or 911.

Module 5 — Long-Term Outcomes · Episode 18

Author’s note (draft): Confirm specifics against your follow-up program before publishing.

Why feeding is hard

Feeding is one of the more common long-term challenges after CDH. Long periods on breathing support, time without feeding by mouth, reflux, and simple fatigue can make it harder for babies to learn to eat. Some develop an aversion to feeding by mouth.

How it is supported

Care often involves feeding therapists and dietitians. Some babies need tube feeding (through the nose or, longer term, through the stomach) for a period while oral feeding develops. Many children outgrow these needs with time and support.

What helps families

Patience and a consistent team matter. Feeding progress can be slow and non-linear, and steady support tends to win out.
