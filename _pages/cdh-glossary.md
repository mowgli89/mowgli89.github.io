> **This page is educational and is not medical advice.** If you have an urgent concern, call your physician or 911.

**CDH (congenital diaphragmatic hernia)** — A hole in the diaphragm, present from birth, that lets abdominal organs move up into the chest and crowd the developing lungs.

**Diaphragm** — The dome-shaped muscle separating the chest from the abdomen; the main muscle of breathing.

**Pulmonary hypoplasia** — Underdeveloped lungs that are smaller and less mature than expected.

**Pulmonary hypertension** — High blood pressure in the lung's blood vessels, which makes it harder to move oxygen into the blood. A central challenge in CDH.

**O/E-TFLV (observed-to-expected total fetal lung volume)** — A fetal MRI measurement comparing your baby's lung volume to what would be expected at that gestational age. Used to estimate severity.

**Percent liver herniation (%LH)** — How much of the liver has moved up into the chest, measured on fetal MRI. More liver up generally means greater severity.

**LHR (lung-to-head ratio)** — An ultrasound-based measure of lung size relative to head size, used at some centers.

**FETO (fetoscopic endoluminal tracheal occlusion)** — A before-birth procedure that places a small balloon in the windpipe to encourage lung growth, removed before delivery.

**Gentle ventilation** — A breathing-support strategy that uses the lowest safe pressures to protect fragile lungs.

**ECMO (extracorporeal membrane oxygenation)** — A heart-lung bypass machine that temporarily does the work of the lungs so they can rest and recover. Used in the most severe cases.

**Patch repair** — Closing a large diaphragm defect with a patch of material when there is not enough tissue to stitch the edges together.

**MFM (maternal-fetal medicine)** — Obstetricians who specialize in high-risk and complex pregnancies.

**Neonatologist** — A physician who specializes in the care of newborns, especially those who are critically ill.
