### Suggested arc
- The big idea: cells feel physical force, not just chemical signals, and force guides how the lung builds itself.
- **PIEZO1** as a stretch-sensitive protein that lets lung cells detect mechanical force.
- How that signal travels inward (calcium, then a regulator called YAP) to influence whether cells grow and which type they become.
- Why this matters for CDH: too little stretch may stall development, and FETO may work by restoring it.
- The bridge to therapy: if we understand the pathway, we might one day work with it directly.

This is the scientific heart of the lab and connects back to [the biology behind fetal lung growth]({{ '/cdh/lung-growth-biology/' | relative_url }}).
