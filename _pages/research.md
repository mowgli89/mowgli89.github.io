The lab's central question is how the developing lung senses and responds to mechanical force, and how that knowledge can improve outcomes in congenital diaphragmatic hernia (CDH).

### Themes

**Mechanosensing in the fetal lung.** We study the PIEZO1 to calcium to YAP pathway as a proximal way that lung epithelial cells convert physical stretch into the signals that direct their development and cell-fate decisions.

**FETO as a biological signal.** Fetal tracheal occlusion is known to encourage lung growth. We are working to understand it as a dose- and duration-dependent biological stimulus, which may help explain why outcomes vary between patients.

**From tissue to model.** Our approaches span primary human neonatal lung cells under controlled stretch, precision-cut lung slices, and developmental models, connected to human spatial transcriptomic data from control, CDH, and FETO-treated lungs.
