*Module 4 — Surgery and Recovery · Episode 16*

### Measured in weeks, not days

Recovery from CDH is usually measured in weeks to months. The diaphragm repair is one milestone; the lungs and circulation still need time to strengthen.

### The usual steps

After surgery, the team gradually weans breathing support as the lungs allow, continues to manage pulmonary hypertension, and works toward feeding. Progress is rarely a straight line, and pauses or small setbacks are common and expected.

### Feeding is often the long pole

Learning to feed by mouth can be one of the slower parts of recovery. Some babies need extra help, such as tube feeding for a time, and feeding challenges are covered in [Module 5]({{ '/cdh/long-term-outcomes/' | relative_url }}).

### Heading home

Discharge comes when the baby is breathing comfortably enough, feeding or being fed safely, and growing. Many families go home with a follow-up plan and, sometimes, some equipment or medicines. Going home is a major milestone, even if the journey continues.
